\section*{Executive Summary}
\addcontentsline{toc}{section}{Executive Summary}
% ============================================================

Understanding how the human spine moves across a full working day or night of sleep
has, until recently, required either a laboratory equipped with optical motion-capture
cameras or a battery of inertial sensors that accumulate drift within minutes of
deployment.
Neither approach yields ecologically valid longitudinal data at the resolution that
spine biomechanics demands.

This document describes and evaluates FlexTail, a wearable spine sensor developed by
MinkTec GmbH \cite{minktec2025} that measures 3D spinal shape continuously by fusing an 18-pair
differential strain-gauge array with a six-axis \ac{IMU} mounted
at the sacrum.
Unlike orientation-based IMU systems, strain gauges directly encode the local curvature
of the substrate at each measurement point; the \ac{DH} kinematic chain then
reconstructs the full 3D spine curve at up to 100\,Hz.

Independent peer-reviewed studies demonstrate:

\begin{itemize}
	\item A mean angular error of \ang{0.8}--\ang{1.1} on standardised in-vitro templates
	      (Masch et al., internal validation, reported in \cite{walkling2025}), comparable
	      to clinical surface measurement devices \cite{spinalMouse}.
	\item A mean F1-score of 0.90 for 11-class human activity recognition at a 1-second
	      window \cite{walkling2025}, outperforming a video-based pose-estimation baseline
	      for trunk-posture transitions (95\,\% vs.\ 76\,\% accuracy for sit-to-stand).
	\item A 60\,\% reduction in time spent in flexed spinal postures when construction
	      workers used a collaborative 3D-printing robot instead of manual concretework,
	      quantified on an active building site where optical motion capture is
	      structurally impractical \cite{sawicki2026}.
\end{itemize}

The system is available in compression-shirt form (lengths 36\,cm or 45\,cm), weighs
30\,g, runs for up to 30\,h per charge, and provides researchers with full raw-data
access either through the internal tooling or exportable is commonly used file formats.
